Description

Boba ("Bubble") tea is a tea-based drink that originated in Taiwan in the early 1980s. It most commonly consists of tea accompanied by sweetened milk and chewy tapioca pearls.

This incredibly refreshing recipe is a joy to make and can be customized with various teas and toppings to your liking!

Ingredients

  • 8 bags of black tea OR 3 tbsp loose-leaf black tea
  • 4 cups hot water
  • 3/4 cup quick-cooking tapioca pearls
  • Whole milk to serve, or your choice of milk
  • simple syrup to serve, or your choice of sweetener

For Simple Syrup:

  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 cup sugar

Directions

Making the Tea:

  1. Steep the tea bags or leaves with 4 cups of freshly boiled water.
  2. Allow the tea to sit in the water until it cools completely.

Making the Simple Syrup:

  1. Add the water and sugar to a saucepan and quickly stir everything together.
  2. Heat the water on medium-high heat and cook until the water boils and the sugar completely dissolves.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let the simple syrup cool before transferring it to a jar.

Cooking the Tapioca Pearls:

  1. Bring about 4 cups of water to boil and add the tapioca pearls.
  2. Stir the pearls and wait for them to float to the top. Then, cook them for another 5 minutes.

    3. *note: Test a pearl to see if it has reached the desired level of softness. Cook the pearls for another few minutes if they are stiff.

  3. Once cooked, drain the hot water from the pearls and quickly rinse them under water before transferring them to a bowl.
  4. Mix the pearls with a few tablespoons of simple syrup.

Assembling the Drinks:

  1. Strain the tea into a pitcher and divide the cooked tapioca pearls into 4 large glasses.
  2. Add a few ice cubes to each glass. Add 1.5 tablespoons of milk and 2.5 tablespoons of simple syrup into each glass. Stir, taste test, and adjust to your liking.

Additional Notes

  • You will likely have some simple syrup left over, which you can refrigerate for 3-4 weeks.
