Kimchi

Description

Kimchi is a traditional Korean side dish of salted and fermented vegetables made with a wide array of seasonings.

This is a great recipe for cooks of all skill levels looking to experiment with making Korean staple foods!

*note: There are various possible substitutions to produce vegan kimchi, but this recipe will be including fish sauce and squid.

Ingredients

  • 10 lbs napa cabbage
  • 1 cup kosher cup
  • 1/2 cup sweet rice flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • water
  • 1 cup crushed garlic
  • 1-2 tbs minced ginger
  • 1 cup minced onion
  • 1 cup fish sauce
  • salty, fermented squid
  • 2.5 cups hot pepper flakes
  • 2 cups chopped leek
  • 10 diagonally sliced green onions
  • 1/4 cup julienned carrots
  • 2 cups julienned Korean radish

Directions

  1. Trim the discolored outer leaves of the napa cabbage.
  2. Cut the cabbage lengthwise into quarters and remove the cores. Chop it up into bite size pieces.
  3. Soak the pieces of cabbage in cold water and put the soaked cabbage into a large basin. Sprinkle with salt.
  4. Every 30 minutes, turn the cabbage over to salt evenly -- total salting time will be 1.5 hours.
  5. 1.5 hours later, rinse the cabbage in cold water 3 times to clean it throroughly.
  6. Drain the cabbage and set it aside.

Making Porridge:

  1. Put 3 cups of water and sweet rice flour in a pot. Mix well and bring to a boil. Keep stirring the porridge until bubbles appear -- approximately 5 minutes.
  2. Add 1/4 cup sugar to the porridge. Stir it and cook for a few more minutes until it becomes translucent.
  3. Remove the porridge from heat and allow it to cool.

Making Kimchi Paste:

  1. Place the cold porridge into a large bowl.
  2. Add fish sauce, hot pepper flakes, crushed garlic, minced ginger, and minced onion.
  3. Wash and drain the fermented squid. Chop it up and add it to the kimchi paste.
  4. Add green onions, chopped leek, Korean radish, and carrots.
  5. Mix all the ingredients thoroughly.

Putting It All Together:

  1. Put the kimchi paste in a large basin and add all the cabbage. Mix it by hand.

    2. *note: If your basin is not large enough to mix all the ingredients at once, do it bit by bit.

  2. Place the kimchi into an air-tight sealed plastic container or glass jar. You can eat it fresh right after making or wait until it's fermented.

Additional notes:

  • You may be wondering how you're supposed to know if the kimchi has fermented or not. To find out, open the lid of the kimchi container 1-2 days after preparation. You may see some bubbles, an increase in liquid, and a sour smell -- this indicates it's already fermenting.
